# Ethnic group → countries of birth expansion

An ethnic/cultural label almost never maps to a single country of birth in Census place-of-birth
data. Expand first, then search for immigrant population by country within that expanded set.
This list is a starting point — extend it as you encounter new target groups, and always sanity-check
with a quick web search since diaspora patterns shift.

## Chinese
Primary: China (PRC), Taiwan, Hong Kong, Macau
Secondary (large ethnic-Chinese minority populations that show up as immigrants from these
countries of birth, not from China): Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam (Hoa people), Indonesia,
Philippines (Chinese-Filipino), Myanmar, Cambodia, Thailand (though Thai Chinese rarely carry
Chinese surnames post-1913 Surname Act — usually not worth including in a surname list even
if population is large).

## Indian
Primary: India
Secondary ("twice-migrant" Indian diaspora — significant in many US metros, especially older/
established South Asian communities): Guyana, Trinidad and Tobago, Fiji, Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania,
United Kingdom (South Asian-British immigrants), Mauritius, South Africa, Suriname.
Note: also consider Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka as *separate* South Asian ethnic groups
if the user says "South Asian" broadly rather than specifically "Indian" — surname pools differ
meaningfully by religion/region even within "Indian" (see surname-sources file).

## Vietnamese
Primary: Vietnam.
Note: surname pool is extremely concentrated (Nguyễn alone is ~40%), so a long ranked list adds
little canvassing value beyond the top ~15-20 names — flag this to the user rather than padding.

## Korean
Primary: South Korea. (North Korean-born immigrants are a negligible population in most US metros.)

## Filipino
Primary: Philippines.
Note: many common Filipino surnames are Spanish (colonial-era, assigned 1849 Claveria decree), not
useful for identifying Chinese-Filipino vs. other Filipino ancestry — see the Chinese-Filipino-specific
surname list in the surname-sources file if the target is specifically Chinese-Filipino rather than
Filipino broadly.

## Mexican / Central American / broader Latino
Primary depends on user specificity: Mexico, Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras, etc. are usually
treated as separate target groups, not lumped — ask if unclear, since canvassing language and
cultural approach differ a lot (Mexican Spanish vs. Central American Spanish communities, plus
indigenous-language-speaking populations e.g. Mixtec, K'iche' in some California/Southern metros).

## When the user names a country directly instead of an ethnicity
E.g. "Punjabi voters" or "Cantonese speakers" — skip the ethnic-group expansion step and go straight
to Step 2 of the main workflow, but still check whether the country/region has meaningful diaspora
sub-populations worth surfacing (e.g. "Punjabi" spans both India and Pakistan).
