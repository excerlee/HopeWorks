# Bay Area ethnic group → language quick reference

Starting points only — always sanity-check against the specific target district, since
generational and dialect mix varies block by block in the Bay Area. When in doubt, search
"[city] [group] population language ACS" for current census language data, or ask the
campaign team which language their target voters actually prefer.

| Target group | Likely primary language(s) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chinese | Mandarin (simplified script for mainland-origin, traditional for Hong Kong/Taiwan-origin); Cantonese (spoken, esp. older/Hong Kong-origin voters) | Older voters and Hong Kong/Guangdong-origin households skew Cantonese; mainland-origin and younger households skew Mandarin. If unsure, include both a Mandarin and Cantonese-friendly written line (written Chinese is largely shared; spoken greeting can differ) |
| Vietnamese | Vietnamese | Fairly uniform; use standard Vietnamese |
| Korean | Korean | Fairly uniform |
| Filipino | English (very high English fluency); Tagalog as supplementary | Often less need for translation than other groups — confirm with local chapter |
| Indian / South Asian | English (common); Hindi, Punjabi, Gujarati, Tamil, Telugu depending on specific community | Highly heterogeneous — ask which specific community/region if possible rather than guessing one language |
| Mexican / Latino | Spanish | Fairly uniform, though indigenous-language speakers (e.g. Mixtec) exist in some communities — flag if relevant |
| Japanese | English (high fluency, esp. later-generation); Japanese for first-generation/Shin-Issei | Smaller, often well-established populations in South Bay |
| Russian / Ukrainian / Eastern European | Russian or Ukrainian depending on origin | Check which is more appropriate given regional politics — don't assume interchangeable |
| Middle Eastern / Afghan / Persian | Farsi/Dari, Arabic depending on origin | Highly heterogeneous — confirm specific community |

## When the district doesn't clearly match one group

Bay Area cities with notably large populations worth checking by name: Fremont, Cupertino,
Milpitas, Daly City, San Jose, Oakland, Union City, Newark, San Mateo, Alameda — each skews
differently (e.g. Fremont has large South Asian + Afghan populations, Daly City has large
Filipino population, Milpitas/Cupertino skew Chinese + South Asian). A quick Census ACS
place-of-birth or language-spoken-at-home search for the specific city is more reliable than
assuming from the city name alone.
